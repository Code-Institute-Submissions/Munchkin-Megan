Toppsta

In 2016 Megan was invited to join Toppsta. Toppsta was launched by Georgina Atwell in Autumn 2014. Mum to two young children, she realised just how hard it was to find books that were age and reading-level appropriate. Determined to provide other parents and children with an easier way to find great books, Toppsta.com was launched. Megan has been a valuable asset to Toppsta, providing nearly two-hundred reviews, with no sign of slowing down. Georgina had this to say about Megan's contributions...

"Megan was one of our very first reviewers back when we first launched in 2016. My dream for Toppsta was to create a space where children could recommend books to each other, to highlight books that children really loved, not just those books which adults think they should be reading. It was clear, pretty early on, that not only was Megan a voracious book reader but that she wrote really helpful and engaging reviews, which appealed to other children. I can’t tell you how much this impressed me - given her age - and she has been an enthusiastic and committed reviewer ever since. I hope she knows that Toppsta would not be the same without her and every recommendation she writes helps other readers. I am truly excited to see where Megan’s reading and writing journey takes her. "
- Georgina Atwell, Toppsta

Scavengers

by Darren Simpson

Wow! What a massive emotional rollercoaster ride of a book. I think I prettymuch cried for the last two chapters! There are so many twists and turns, and each one is brilliant. This is ridiculously wellwritten book and I loved reading every minute of it.

I found myself frustrated, sad, angry, happy and sometimes all within a short space of time. The character I most relate to is Dawn because she's very caring and adventurous, constantly wanting to help Landfill. It completely peaked my curiosity and I couldn't put it down. I would re-read it in a heart beat! That ending...... The blurb does not do this book justice!

Jinxed

by Amy McCulloch

Well, I absolutely loved and I mean LOVED, this book! It had me gripped from the start, and I thought the idea of the Bakus was excellent! I genuinely want one! I felt very much for Lacey when times were tough, I thought the characterisation and their development was executed brilliantly!

Amy did a superb job writing this book, I couldn't put it down and whenever I was reading this book, I found it incredibly immersive, like I was actually there! A mixture of sci fi and mystery, this book was a thrilling read from start to finish and I look forward whole heartedly to the next one!

In the Key of Code

by Aimee Lucido

I loved this book, it was just amazing! I adored the layout, it was like reading music or poetry. It felt like there was a symphony in my head and I've never read anything that has done that before. The story telling and the character description was sublime.

It is one of the BEST books that I have read in a long time. I wanted to read the book because I code, I enjoy coding and find it fun so I liked that element to the story. BUT I got so much more than I bargained for, it's like you're in the middle of symphony or an opera. I LOVED IT!

Gaslight

by Eloise Williams

Genuinely an interesting and engaging read. Somewhere between the dark side of existence, mystery and intrigue. I haven't been able to put it down and when I have I've felt Eloise Williams pulling me back in, whispering that I haven't quite finished the story yet. Deeply enthralling, I find myself fully immersed in the era, the setting and standing firmly beside Nansi- who is very likeable and enjoyable to read about.

The Boy Who Flew

by Fleur Hitchcock

The Boy who Flew was an incredible read and I couldn't have wished for more. Although, like with most great books, I really did wish there was more of it to read! If i'm honest, I wasn't sure at first but Fleur won me around with her stellar writing- it got interesting!! Within a couple of chapters I was absolutely hooked. One night the intrigue was coming so thick and fast that I read 15 chapters and stayed up well passed my bed time! There were so many times that I cried, screamed and laughed. There are so incredibly tense moments and you feel fully immersed in the story. There was no chance to catch my breath between emotions and I loved every minute of it. With everything that went into this amazing book, I really have to thank Fleur for such a heartfelt story. A thrill for all.
