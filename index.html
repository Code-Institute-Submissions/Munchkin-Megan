About Megan

Twelve-year-old Megan is a prolific reader and has been reading since she was just one! Never without a book in her hand, or her heart, she can be frequently found in bookshops, charity shops and at her local library, where is a proud member of ‘chatter-books’, a group designed to help encourage children to put down their devices and pick up a book.

Megan is a keen supporter of literacy and authors alike, and now she is older, is probably reading a little less but more substantial literature, having recentley finishing 'Good Omens' by Neil Gaiman and Terry Pratchett. Generally, Megan reads several times a day, and estimates to have read well over 2500 books!

Books have no limits, no borders and what you read is entirely your choice, there is so much to choose from. Books provide freedom, adventure, and community spirit that, until recently, seems to have been forgotten in the electronic age. Megan’s book selections cross age brackets and genres, and as a keen reviewer, Megan has decided to share her journey through books with other people. She is confident in her approach to what she has read, what she likes and dislikes, and she has no problem expressing those feelings with other booklovers.

Through her love of books, Megan is also a keen writer and has already written numerous books, finishing her first book at just seven-years-old! She plans to continue writing until she joins all her fellow author peers on the shelves of bookstores and libraries. When Megan is not reading (or writing), she enjoys playing the piano, drawing, baking, and swimming.

I am not a bookworm

When I start a new book I feel like I have entered a completely different world which means adventure, mystery and excitement throughout. I am transported to different places and make new friends along the way. A good writer makes you forget about the real world and makes you feel like you’re part of the furniture - that is to say that their welcome embrace makes you feel right at home. A new book is a gateway between my world and the land of the author’s brain - what a weird and wacky place that is!

I feel depressed when I finish a book, fellow book folk will understand that inevitable deflation upon completing a book. No matter how long you’ve been reading it, whether it be a few hours, a day or a week, you have invested yourself in this world and this world has completely enveloped you. When it ends you’re forced to say goodbye to the characters you’ve come to know, support and admire. That really does make an impact and I question “What am I going to do now?” but thankfully that can be easily rectified by delving into another book and getting completely swept up in another world, another life. I get to travel the world, time and the universe. I am not a bookworm, I am a book traveler - a book hitch hiker and I get to visit all the places you have ever dreamed of.

365 book challenge

At just five-years-old, Megan challenged herself to read 365 books in twelve months and completed her task in just ten! She wrote to the childrens television show Blue Peter, describing her reading accomplishments to see if she could get a Blue Peter badge. Megan could hardly believe it when Blue Peter presenter, Lindsey Russell turned up at her local library to present her with her very own BP badge.

Since winning her first badge, Megan has gone on to build up a collection of fourteen badges, making sereval appearances on the show. Most recently Megan recieved her orange badge as an age group winner in a competition to design a garden sculpture for HRH Duchess of Cambridge at RHS Wisley. Megan was invited to RHS Wisley along with the other category winners to help the duchess reveal the winning sculpture. However, her first badge will always be her most special.
